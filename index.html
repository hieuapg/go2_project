Robotic Assistant for Safe Patient Mobility Through Oxygen Cable Management

Abstract

Long-term oxygen therapy (LTOT) patients often rely on portable tanks connected via nasal cannulae or tubing, which introduces mobility constraints and tripping hazards. We present a mobile robotic system that actively regulates oxygen cable slack while following the patient, improving safety during daily activities. The system combines a Unitree Go2 quadruped, a lightweight 6-DoF arm with a hook-shaped gripper, dual Intel RealSense D435 cameras with vertical image stitching for wide-field RGB-D perception, YOLO-Pose for patient localization, and a Hesai XT-16 LiDAR for obstacle detection. A predictive controller is proposed to anticipate patient transitions (sit–stand–walk) and reduce tension spikes by dynamically routing the cable. We plan to evaluate the system in a clinical-like environment to assess its ability to maintain safe slack, avoid obstacles, and follow patient motion. This work aims to demonstrate the feasibility of deploying perception-driven, predictive cable management for LTOT patients in hospital or home settings.
